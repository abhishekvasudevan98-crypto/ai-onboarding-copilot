1. Purpose & Governance Authority

This protocol defines how Honey’s Consulting Group manages internal investigations, whistleblower reports, and regulatory allegations.

The framework ensures investigations are conducted in a structured, impartial, and legally defensible manner.

The objectives of this protocol are to:

• Ensure impartial and legally defensible investigations
• Protect whistleblower confidentiality
• Mitigate regulatory and reputational risk
• Maintain executive and board oversight
• Ensure proper documentation and evidentiary control

This protocol is accessible only to authorized Compliance personnel and designated executive leadership.

2. Trigger Events for Investigation

An internal investigation may begin when potential misconduct, regulatory violations, or ethical concerns are identified.

Common trigger events include:

• Whistleblower hotline report
• Anonymous ethics submission
• Internal audit findings
• Executive leadership directive
• Regulatory inquiry
• Suspicious financial activity

All trigger events must be formally recorded in the Compliance Case Management System.

3. Case Intake & Logging Procedure

All investigations must be formally registered before investigative activity begins.

3.1 Case Registration

Each investigation is assigned the following:

• Unique Case ID
• Date of initiation
• Risk classification
• Assigned Compliance Officer

3.2 Preliminary Assessment

Within 5 business days, the assigned Compliance Officer must:

• Assess credibility of the allegation
• Evaluate potential regulatory exposure
• Identify immediate containment actions

3.3 Conflict Screening

Investigators must confirm they have no conflict of interest related to the case.

Investigators with potential conflicts must recuse themselves.

4. Evidence Preservation & Handling

Evidence must be preserved in a secure and legally defensible manner.

4.1 Digital Evidence

Digital evidence must be:

• Preserved in a secure evidence repository
• Access restricted
• Timestamped and logged

4.2 Physical Evidence

Physical documents must be:

• Logged in the evidence registry
• Stored in a secure location
• Access controlled

4.3 Chain of Custody

A documented chain of custody must be maintained for all evidence collected.

Failure to preserve evidence properly may compromise the legal defensibility of the investigation.

5. Investigation Process Framework

Investigations follow a structured multi-phase process.

5.1 Planning Phase

During planning investigators must:

• Define the scope of the investigation
• Identify stakeholders
• Determine required documentation

5.2 Interview Phase

Interviews may be conducted with:

• Reporting party
• Subject of allegation
• Relevant witnesses

All interviews must be documented in written investigation records.

5.3 Legal Consultation

If legal exposure exists, the Compliance team must consult Legal Counsel.

Legal counsel ensures investigations comply with regulatory and legal obligations.

6. Risk Classification & Escalation

Cases are classified based on risk severity.

Classification levels include:

Low Risk – Internal policy deviation
Medium Risk – Material policy violation
High Risk – Significant legal or financial exposure
Critical Risk – Immediate executive or board escalation required

High and Critical cases must be escalated within 48 hours to:

• Chief Compliance Officer
• General Counsel
• Chief Executive Officer

Critical cases may also require notification of the Board Audit Committee.

7. Whistleblower Protections

Honey’s Consulting Group protects individuals who report concerns.

7.1 Confidentiality

Whistleblower identities must remain confidential unless disclosure is legally required.

7.2 Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against whistleblowers is strictly prohibited.

Employees who retaliate against reporting individuals may face disciplinary action.

7.3 Monitoring

Compliance will monitor the workplace environment to detect potential retaliation.

8. Investigation Closure & Reporting

Once an investigation concludes, formal reporting must occur.

8.1 Findings Report

Each investigation must produce a written findings report including:

• Summary of allegations
• Evidence analysis
• Interview summaries
• Risk classification
• Recommended actions

8.2 Disciplinary Action

If misconduct is confirmed, actions may include:

• Formal warning
• Suspension
• Termination
• Referral to law enforcement

8.3 Regulatory Disclosure

Where legally required, findings may be reported to regulatory authorities.

9. Record Retention & Confidential Storage

Investigation records must be securely stored.

Requirements include:

• Minimum 10-year retention period
• Access restricted to authorized Compliance leadership
• Secure enterprise storage systems

10. Policy Review & Governance Oversight

This protocol is reviewed annually by:

• Chief Compliance Officer
• General Counsel

Material policy updates require executive approval.

Quick Answers (AI Retrieval Section)

Q: What triggers an internal investigation?
A: Internal investigations may be triggered by whistleblower reports, anonymous ethics submissions, internal audit findings, regulatory inquiries, or suspicious financial activity.

Q: Who manages internal investigations at the company?
A: Internal investigations are managed by the Compliance Department under the supervision of the Chief Compliance Officer.

Q: How quickly must preliminary assessments occur?
A: Preliminary investigation assessments must be completed within 5 business days.

Q: What happens if a case is classified as High Risk?
A: High risk cases must be escalated within 48 hours to the Chief Compliance Officer, General Counsel, and CEO.

Q: Are whistleblowers protected from retaliation?
A: Yes. The company strictly prohibits retaliation against individuals who report concerns in good faith.

Q: How long are investigation records retained?
A: Investigation records must be retained for a minimum of 10 years.
