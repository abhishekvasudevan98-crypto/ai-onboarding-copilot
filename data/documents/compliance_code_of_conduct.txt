1. Purpose & Ethical Commitment

Honey’s Consulting Group (“HCG”) is committed to conducting business with integrity, transparency, and accountability.

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical standards, behavioral expectations, and regulatory compliance requirements applicable to all employees, contractors, and executive leadership.

This document explains the company’s rules regarding ethical behavior, professional conduct, and regulatory compliance.

The objectives of this Code are to:

• Promote ethical decision-making
• Prevent misconduct and regulatory violations
• Protect client trust and corporate reputation
• Ensure compliance with applicable laws and regulations
• Establish reporting and accountability mechanisms

All employees must review and acknowledge this Code annually.

2. Scope & Applicability

The Code of Conduct applies to all individuals working with or representing Honey’s Consulting Group.

This includes:

• Full-time employees
• Part-time employees
• Contract personnel
• Executive leadership
• Board members

Compliance with this Code is mandatory regardless of role, geography, or tenure.

3. Standards of Professional Conduct

Employees must maintain high professional standards when conducting business on behalf of HCG.

3.1 Integrity & Honesty

Employees must act honestly and avoid deceptive practices in all business dealings.

Misrepresentation, fraud, or intentional misinformation is strictly prohibited.

3.2 Professional Responsibility

All work performed must meet professional standards consistent with industry best practices.

Employees must maintain accuracy, diligence, and accountability in their responsibilities.

3.3 Respectful Workplace

Honey’s Consulting Group maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward workplace misconduct.

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

• Harassment
• Discrimination
• Retaliation
• Workplace misconduct

All employees are expected to contribute to a respectful and inclusive workplace.

3.4 Confidentiality Obligations

Employees must safeguard confidential information belonging to:

• Clients
• Employees
• Honey’s Consulting Group

Unauthorized disclosure or misuse of confidential information is strictly prohibited.

4. Anti-Bribery & Anti-Corruption

Honey’s Consulting Group maintains strict compliance with anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws.

4.1 Prohibition on Bribery

Employees may not offer, promise, authorize, or accept bribes, kickbacks, or improper payments.

Any attempt to influence business decisions through improper payments is strictly prohibited.

4.2 Gifts & Entertainment

Business gifts and entertainment may be acceptable if they meet the following criteria:

• Reasonable in value
• Not intended to influence business decisions
• Consistent with company policy and client regulations

Excessive or inappropriate gifts must be declined or reported.

4.3 Third-Party Interactions

Employees must ensure that third parties acting on behalf of HCG comply with anti-corruption laws and ethical standards.

Violations involving third parties may expose the company to legal risk.

5. Conflicts of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests.

5.1 Disclosure Requirement

Employees must disclose any potential conflict of interest to the Compliance Office.

5.2 Outside Employment

Secondary employment that creates competitive risk or confidentiality concerns must be disclosed and approved.

5.3 Financial Interests

Employees must report ownership interests in competitors, suppliers, or vendors.

The Compliance Office will evaluate and determine appropriate mitigation steps.

6. Financial Integrity & Reporting

Employees must maintain accurate and truthful financial reporting practices.

This includes:

• Accurate reporting of financial transactions
• Honest expense submissions
• Proper documentation of business records

Intentional falsification of financial records constitutes serious misconduct.

7. Data Protection & Privacy

Honey’s Consulting Group requires strict protection of confidential and sensitive data.

Employees must comply with applicable data protection laws and internal security policies.

Sensitive information must only be stored and shared through authorized company systems.

Use of personal storage platforms for confidential information is prohibited.

8. Reporting & Whistleblower Protections

Employees are encouraged to report suspected misconduct or ethical concerns.

8.1 Reporting Channels

Employees may report concerns through the following channels:

• Direct manager
• Compliance Office
• Anonymous reporting hotline
• Designated ethics email channel

8.2 Non-Retaliation Policy

Honey’s Consulting Group strictly prohibits retaliation against individuals who report concerns in good faith.

Employees who report violations will be protected from adverse employment consequences.

8.3 Investigation Process

All credible allegations are reviewed by the Compliance Department.

Investigations are conducted confidentially and objectively.

9. Disciplinary Framework

Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in disciplinary action.

Possible actions include:

• Formal warning
• Suspension
• Termination
• Legal action where applicable

The severity of the disciplinary action depends on the nature and impact of the violation.

10. Governance Oversight & Review

The Compliance Department is responsible for monitoring adherence to this Code.

Responsibilities include:

• Conducting periodic ethics training
• Monitoring regulatory compliance
• Reporting material compliance issues to executive leadership

This document is reviewed annually and updated as regulatory requirements evolve.
