Access is restricted to authorized Finance personnel and executive leadership.

Unauthorized access or disclosure may result in disciplinary action.

1. Purpose & Compensation Governance Principles

This protocol defines how Honey’s Consulting Group manages salary adjustments, compensation reviews, and executive pay governance.

The document establishes how compensation decisions are evaluated, approved, and recorded.

The objectives of this protocol are to:

• Ensure equitable and merit-based compensation decisions
• Maintain internal pay equity standards
• Align compensation with employee performance and market benchmarks
• Protect confidentiality of employee salary information
• Maintain fiscal discipline within approved compensation budgets

All compensation adjustments must align with approved financial forecasts and departmental budget allocations.

2. Annual Compensation Review Cycle

Honey’s Consulting Group conducts a formal compensation review once per year.

2.1 Review Timing

The formal compensation review cycle occurs annually during Q4.

Salary adjustments approved during the annual cycle typically take effect in the following fiscal period.

2.2 Participants

The following roles participate in the compensation review process:

• Direct Managers
• Department Heads
• Finance Director
• Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

2.3 Eligibility Criteria

Employees must meet the following criteria to be eligible for salary review:

• At least 6 months of employment service
• Completed performance review documentation
• No active disciplinary review status

3. Performance-Based Adjustment Framework

Salary adjustments are determined using performance-based evaluation tiers.

3.1 Performance Tier Classification

Employees are categorized into the following performance tiers:

Tier 1 – Exceptional Performance
Tier 2 – Strong Performance
Tier 3 – Meets Expectations
Tier 4 – Improvement Required

3.2 Standard Adjustment Guidelines

Typical salary increase ranges are:

Tier 1: 6–8% salary increase
Tier 2: 4–6% salary increase
Tier 3: 3–4% salary increase
Tier 4: No increase or performance improvement plan

Salary adjustments above 7% require CFO approval.

Adjustments outside defined ranges require written approval from the CFO.

4. Market Benchmarking Standards

The Finance Department conducts compensation benchmarking annually.

Benchmarking includes analysis of:

• Industry salary surveys
• Geographic cost-of-living data
• Competitor compensation levels

These benchmarks help ensure that company salaries remain competitive and aligned with the external market.

All benchmarking methodology must be formally documented.

5. Approval Workflow

Salary adjustments must follow a structured approval process.

5.1 Department Recommendation

Managers submit recommended salary adjustments along with written justification.

5.2 Finance Review

The Finance Department validates:

• Budget availability
• Internal pay equity
• Historical salary progression

5.3 CFO Approval

The Chief Financial Officer has final approval authority for:

• Executive-level compensation
• Salary increases exceeding 7%
• Off-cycle salary adjustments

6. Off-Cycle Adjustments

Off-cycle salary adjustments may occur outside the annual compensation cycle.

Common reasons include:

• Employee promotion
• Role expansion
• Retention risk
• Market compensation correction

Off-cycle adjustments require written business justification and CFO approval.

7. Confidentiality & Data Protection

Salary data is classified as Restricted confidential information.

The following rules apply:

• Compensation discussions must remain confidential
• Salary data must not be exported outside approved HR systems
• Access is limited to authorized Finance and HR leadership

Unauthorized disclosure of compensation data may result in disciplinary action or termination.

8. Payroll System Integration

All approved salary adjustments must be processed through official payroll systems.

The company uses the following systems:

• Workday HRIS system
• Corporate payroll processing system

Finance must verify that payroll systems reflect approved compensation changes before payroll processing.

9. Audit & Compliance Oversight

Internal Audit may review compensation governance processes including:

• Compensation approval workflows
• Budget adherence
• Pay equity analysis

Any irregularities must be escalated immediately to the Chief Financial Officer.

10. Policy Review & Governance Maintenance

This protocol must be reviewed annually by:

• Finance Director
• Chief Financial Officer
