1. Purpose & Governance Mandate

This policy defines the official enterprise systems used at Honey’s Consulting Group (“HCG”) and establishes the rules for system access, data protection, and acceptable use.

The document explains what systems employees must use for business activities and how access to those systems is managed.

The objectives of this policy are to:

• Safeguard corporate and client data
• Ensure operational continuity and cybersecurity resilience
• Establish consistent access control standards
• Prevent unauthorized system usage
• Align with industry security frameworks and regulatory expectations

All employees must comply with this policy as a condition of system access.

The Information Technology Department oversees all enterprise systems and may suspend system access for policy violations.

2. Enterprise Systems Landscape

Honey’s Consulting Group maintains several official enterprise platforms used for internal operations.

Employees must use these approved systems to perform their work.

2.1 Human Resources Information System (HRIS) – Workday

Workday is the company’s official HR system.

Employees use Workday for:

• Payroll processing
• Leave management
• Employee records
• Performance documentation

Access to Workday is role-based and managed jointly by the HR and IT departments.

2.2 Expense Management System – Expensify

Expensify is the company’s official system for managing employee expenses.

Employees use Expensify for:

• Expense submission
• Reimbursement tracking
• Expense approval workflows

The Expensify platform integrates with the Finance Department’s expense approval process.

2.3 Customer Relationship Management (CRM) – Salesforce

Salesforce is the company’s official customer relationship management system.

Employees use Salesforce for:

• Client opportunity tracking
• Revenue forecasting
• Account management documentation

Access to Salesforce data is restricted based on job role and business function.

2.4 Project & Risk Management – Jira

Jira is the company’s official project and risk management platform.

Employees use Jira for:

• Task tracking
• Risk logging
• Escalation documentation
• Project milestone reporting

Jira serves as the official repository for operational risk documentation.

2.5 Knowledge Repository – Confluence

Confluence is the company’s official knowledge management platform.

Employees use Confluence for:

• Internal policy storage
• Standard operating procedures
• Department documentation
• Version-controlled internal guidance

Confluence is considered the authoritative source for internal company documentation.

3. Access Control & Identity Governance

System access is managed through formal access control processes.

3.1 Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Access to systems is granted based on:

• Job role
• Department
• Business necessity

Employees may not request elevated access outside their responsibilities without approval from both their department head and IT Security.

3.2 Principle of Least Privilege

Employees are granted only the minimum level of access required to perform their work duties.

3.3 Access Reviews

IT Security conducts quarterly access reviews to ensure system permissions remain appropriate.

3.4 Offboarding Controls

When an employee leaves the company:

• All system access must be revoked within 24 hours
• Corporate devices must be returned
• Password credentials must be invalidated

4. Security Requirements
4.1 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for all enterprise systems.

4.2 VPN Requirement

Remote system access requires use of the company’s approved corporate VPN.

4.3 Password Standards

Passwords must meet corporate complexity standards and may not be reused across systems.

4.4 Device Security

Corporate-issued devices must comply with endpoint security standards including:

• Full disk encryption
• Automatic security updates
• Anti-malware protection

5. Acceptable Use Standards

Employees must use enterprise systems only for legitimate business purposes.

The following actions are prohibited:

• Unauthorized data extraction
• Sharing system credentials
• Accessing confidential data outside job scope
• Installing unauthorized software
• Using corporate systems for personal commercial activities

The IT Department may monitor system activity to ensure compliance.

6. Data Classification & Handling

Honey’s Consulting Group uses four levels of data classification.

6.1 Data Classification Levels

• Public
• Internal
• Confidential
• Restricted

6.2 Storage Requirements

Confidential and Restricted data must only be stored in approved enterprise systems.

Storing sensitive data on personal devices is prohibited unless authorized and encrypted.

6.3 Data Transmission

Sensitive information must not be transmitted using unsecured communication channels.

7. Incident Response & Reporting

Employees must immediately report potential security incidents.

Examples include:

• Suspected phishing attempts
• Unauthorized system access
• Lost or stolen devices
• Data breaches or accidental disclosure

Reports should be submitted to:

• IT Security Team
• Direct Manager
• Compliance Office when regulatory implications exist

Failure to report incidents may result in disciplinary action.

8. Compliance & Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:

• Temporary suspension of system access
• Formal disciplinary review
• Termination of employment
• Legal action where applicable

The IT Department may audit system usage logs to detect violations.

9. Policy Maintenance

This policy is reviewed annually by IT Security leadership.

Material updates require approval from the Chief Technology Officer.

All policy versions are maintained within the company’s Confluence documentation system.
