1. Purpose & Governance Framework

This policy defines the official rules for business expense reimbursement and corporate spending at Honey’s Consulting Group (“HCG”).

The policy explains how employees submit business expenses, what expenses are reimbursable, and how approvals are handled.

The objectives of this policy are to:

• Ensure responsible stewardship of company financial resources
• Promote transparency and accountability in expense reporting
• Mitigate financial fraud and misuse of corporate funds
• Align with internal audit controls and regulatory expectations
• Maintain consistency across global business units

All employees are responsible for understanding and complying with this policy.

Failure to follow the policy may result in:

• Reimbursement denial
• Disciplinary action
• Termination in severe cases

This policy operates under the oversight of the Finance Department and is subject to periodic review by Internal Audit.

2. Scope & Applicability

This policy applies to all individuals who submit expenses through the company’s expense reimbursement system.

This includes:

• Full-time employees
• Part-time employees
• Contract personnel reimbursed through corporate systems
• Executive leadership
• International employees operating under the HCG reimbursement framework

Local legal requirements may impose additional documentation standards, but this policy defines the minimum global standard.

3. Guiding Principles of Expense Management

The expense management process follows several key principles.

3.1 Business Necessity Standard

All expenses must be directly related to legitimate business activities.

Examples include:

• Client delivery work
• Operational business travel
• Professional development aligned with job responsibilities

Expenses unrelated to business activities will not be reimbursed.

3.2 Reasonableness Standard

Expenses must reflect prudent judgment consistent with a professional consulting environment.

Excessive or luxury spending requires documented pre-approval.

3.3 Timeliness Requirement

Employees must submit expenses within 45 days of the expense being incurred.

If submitted after 45 days, the employee must provide:

• Written justification
• Direct manager approval
• Secondary Finance review

Repeated late submissions may result in reimbursement denial.

3.4 Accuracy & Completeness

Employees are responsible for ensuring expense reports include:

• Accurate expense categorization
• Clear explanation of business purpose
• Receipts and supporting documentation
• Correct currency conversions for international expenses

3.5 Segregation of Duties

Employees may not approve their own expenses.

Expense approvals must follow the company’s official approval matrix described in Section 8.

4. Eligible Expense Categories

Subject to policy compliance, the following expense categories are generally reimbursable.

4.1 Travel & Transportation

• Economy-class airfare
• Standard hotel accommodations
• Ground transportation such as taxis, rideshare services, or trains
• Mileage reimbursement at the approved corporate rate
• Visa fees required for business travel

4.2 Client Development & Meetings

• Client meals within reasonable limits
• Approved entertainment associated with client engagement
• Meeting room rentals for business meetings

4.3 Professional Development

• Pre-approved industry certifications
• Training programs aligned with employee role requirements
• Professional conference attendance

4.4 Business Supplies & Equipment

• Minor office supplies
• Project-related materials
• Approved technology peripherals

Major equipment purchases require separate procurement approval.

5. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are not reimbursable unless explicitly approved by executive leadership:

• Personal entertainment unrelated to business
• Alcohol not tied to approved client events
• Traffic violations or parking fines
• First-class or business-class airfare without CFO approval
• Family travel expenses
• Personal subscription services

The Finance Department retains final authority in determining reimbursement eligibility.

6. Corporate Card Usage
6.1 Mandatory Use

When corporate cards are issued, employees must use them for eligible business expenses.

6.2 Reconciliation Requirements

Corporate card statements must be reconciled monthly within the company’s expense management system.

6.3 Misuse of Corporate Cards

Improper use of corporate cards may result in:

• Revocation of corporate card privileges
• Financial recovery actions
• Disciplinary measures

7. Travel & Booking Controls
7.1 Pre-Approval Requirement

All business travel must receive documented manager approval before booking.

7.2 Approved Booking Channels

Employees must use approved corporate travel platforms unless an exception is granted.

7.3 International Travel Risk Review

International travel requires additional review for:

• Insurance coverage
• Compliance risk exposure
• Data security considerations

8. Approval Matrix

Expense approval authority is based on the following spending thresholds:

• Under $1,000 → Direct Manager approval
• $1,000–$5,000 → Department Head approval
• Above $5,000 → Chief Financial Officer approval

Finance may escalate any expense for additional review regardless of amount.

9. Audit, Monitoring & Fraud Prevention
9.1 Audit Rights

The Finance Department and Internal Audit may conduct random or targeted expense audits.

9.2 Fraud Detection

Intentional misrepresentation of expenses constitutes financial misconduct.

9.3 Consequences

Violations may result in:

• Reimbursement denial
• Written warning
• Termination
• Legal action where applicable

10. Policy Maintenance & Review

This policy is reviewed annually by Finance leadership.

Material policy updates require CFO approval.

Policy updates are communicated to employees and maintained in the corporate document repository.
